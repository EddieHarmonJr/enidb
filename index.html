Our Story Shop

Welcome! Thank you for stopping by Enid B. where we specialize in creating artisanal body butters, foot soaks, lip balms, scrubs, shampoos, and soaps. All carefully handcrafted with great care and research to provide benefit to your skin using amazing ingrdients.

Our Ethos

We value people above profit. This is why we strive to use:

Handcrafted

All products are made with love and care by hand. Passion goes into every product we produce.

Sustainable

We aim to leave a small, eco-friendly footprint that helps make the world a better place to live in.

Natural/Organic

Whenever possible, we use organic ingredients. If unavailable, we use the finest, natural ingredients we can find.

Meet The Founder

I started Enid B. with a passion to make products that were less affected by the ins-and-outs of industrialization. In short, a passion to make products that we wouldn't be afraid to use ourselves (because of the harsh dyes, chemicals, and other "mystery ingredients" that were contained in them). This gave rise to a desire to do it independently.

This company is inspired by my grandmother, Enid Barnes and is a tribute to her legacy. A resourceful woman who was sustainable before it became a trendy topic, who first introduced me to herbal medicine.

Before this company was officially started, I began creating products based on the needs of myself, my family and friends. When they tried my products, I was encouraged to keep producing them, and my customer base grew.

My vision is to craft products that "Take Care Of You, So That You Can Care For Others"™

What Goes Inside Enid B. Products?

We put ingredients that you can see and pronounce inside! We stay away from harsh, synthetic chemicals that damage the skin and body. We don't use unnecessary dyes, fragrances or preservatives in our products.

We create products that are well researched, using ingredients that are sourced with the least amount of processing to produce the finished product. We use herbs that are wild crafted (found naturally in nature), some herbs that are grown organically on farms and some that are conventionally grown.
